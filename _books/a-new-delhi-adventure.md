Dino and Benny say namaste in marigold garlands, then set off through a city of red stone and bright color. They stand before a grand gate that remembers brave soldiers, zoom through traffic in a green and yellow auto, and ring the bell of a cycle rickshaw down a busy little lane. There are bangles that shine in red and gold and blue, hot paratha puffing up on the pan, sticky orange swirls of jalebi, and a spice market so fragrant it makes Dino sneeze.

Then the day opens out. Dino sends a kite dancing over the rooftops, and the metro whooshes them across the city with Benny reading the map. They crane their necks at the tallest brick tower in the world, find quiet inside a building shaped like a white flower, and watch a peacock spread his tail into a hundred shiny eyes. When night comes, it is Diwali: little lamps glow in rows, colored sand becomes a rangoli flower, and the whole city sparkles.
