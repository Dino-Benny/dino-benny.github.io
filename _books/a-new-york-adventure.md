Dino and Benny tip their heads all the way back to look at buildings that touch the sky. They count subway stops, find golden stars painted on a train station ceiling, and sail out on the ferry to meet the Statue of Liberty, whose crown has seven points and who came all the way from France. They walk the long ropes and tall towers of the Brooklyn Bridge, then learn to fold a big slice of pizza the way New Yorkers do.

The day keeps climbing. A rowboat on the lake, a spinning carousel, a museum where Dino waves at a dinosaur skeleton and calls it cousin. There are stone lions at the library, a warm soft pretzel from a cart, and an elevator that races up 102 floors until the whole city looks small. Then the lights blink on, a street drummer taps out a beat, and two friends head home with tired feet and happy hearts.
